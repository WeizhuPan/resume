A Replication Study: Underestimating the Positive Impact of Compliments Creates a Barrier to Expressing Them

03-06/2023 | Project Leader | Advisor: Prof. Fang Wang

  • Adapted the experimental design to fit the Chinese cultural context based on the original research on the impact of compliments on recipients, ensuring more effective testing of whether people underestimate the positive impact of compliments in a local environment
  • Prepared survey questionnaires with 12 questions and experimental guidelines for a field experiment at BNU, covering participants’ emotional responses, givers’ expectations of recipients’ perceptions, relationship closeness, and the frequency of compliment exchanges
  • Randomly assigned participants as givers or receivers, instructing givers to write three specific compliments of at least eight characters, which were later read by the receivers
  • Collected and analyzed 86 survey questionnaires using SPSS to assess the impact of compliments on recipients
  • Analyzed experimental data and authored a report, finding significant differences in results that reflect cultural variations and characteristics of the participant group
